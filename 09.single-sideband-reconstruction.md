:::::{tab-set}
::::{tab-item} Ideal acquisition

In the absence of aberrations, $\chi(\bm{k}) = 0$ results in two redundant overlap regions.
Equation @eq_forward_model can then be inverted using SSB ptychography [@https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2014.09.013]

:::{math}
:label: eq_ssb_estimator
\phi_{\mathrm{SSB}}(\bm{q}) = \sum_{\bm{k}} A(\bm{k}) A(\bm{k-q}) G(\bm{q,k})
::: 

::::

::::{tab-item} Aberrated acquisition
In the presence of aberrations, the probe overlaps acquire a phase structure.
Equation @eq_forward_model is subsequently inverted using a phase-compensated kernel [@https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2016.09.002]

:::{math}
:label: eq_ssb_phase_compensated_estimator
\phi_{\mathrm{SSB}'}(\bm{q}) = \sum_{\bm{k}} \frac{\Gamma^*(\bm{q,k})}{|\Gamma(\bm{q,k})|} G(\bm{q,k})
:::
