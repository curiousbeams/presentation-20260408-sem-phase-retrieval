:::::{tab-set}
::::{tab-item} Quadratic approximation
This is similar to parallax imaging or tilt-corrected BF STEM (tcBF).
Specifically, it represents a quadratic SSB ptychography kernel approximation [@10.48550/arXiv.2507.18610]

:::{math}
:label: eq_gamma_approx
\begin{align*}
\Gamma(\bm{q,k}) &\approx \mathrm{e}^{-\mathrm{i} \nabla_{\bm{k}} \chi(\bm{k}) \cdot \bm{q}} \\
\phi_{\mathrm{prlx}}(\bm{q}) &= \sum_{\bm{k} : |\bm{k}| \lt k_0 } \mathrm{e}^{-\mathrm{i} \nabla_{\bm{k}} \chi(\bm{k}) \cdot \bm{q}} \; G(\bm{q,k})
\end{align*}
:::

::::
::::{tab-item} Contrast reversal
Parallax imaging exhibits contrast reversals and yields BF-like contrast.
A global phase-flip kernel provides an efficient approximation of the $\Gamma(\bm{q,k})$ parity

:::{math}
:label: eq_phase_flip
\phi_{\mathrm{prlx}'}(\bm{q}) = \sum_{\bm{k} : |\bm{k}| \lt k_0 } \mathrm{e}^{-\mathrm{i} \nabla_{\bm{k}} \chi(\bm{k}) \cdot \bm{q}} \; \mathrm{sgn}\{\sin[\chi(\bm{q})]\} \;  G(\bm{q,k})
:::
